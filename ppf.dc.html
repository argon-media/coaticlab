What Is Paint Protection Film?

Paint protection film is a lightweight urethane layer applied over your vehicle's painted surfaces. The film is virtually invisible once installed, and it acts as a shield against rock chips, bugs, hard water, bird droppings, chemical stains, and UV rays.

It works as a sacrificial layer. When a pebble kicks up off the highway, the film absorbs the impact instead of your paint. The chip that would have gone into your clear coat goes into the film.

The technology is older than most people realize. It goes back to the Vietnam War, when 3M developed a film to protect military helicopter rotor blades from debris damage. The same principle protects your hood today.

What the Film Actually Does

Self-Healing Topcoat

Light scratches and swirl marks disappear with heat. Park it in the sun or run warm water over the panel and the film re-levels itself.

Damage Prevention

Film blocks hard objects and debris from reaching your paint, saving you from nicks, chips, stains, and scratches.

Non-Yellowing

The film stays clear. It will not yellow from UV exposure, or from the solvents in bird droppings, gas, and oil.

Edge Seal Technology

Most film peels at the edges over time. Ours is sealed tight to the paint with our signature edge seal technology, so nothing gets underneath.

Stain Resistance

Hard water spots, mineral deposits, and acidic contaminants stop being a problem.

10-Year Warranty

Our film is backed by a 10-year manufacturer’s warranty.

Paint Protection Film FAQ

Will the film be visible?+

Gloss film is virtually invisible once installed. It carries your paint color straight through and reads as factory finish.

How long does paint protection film last?+

Professionally installed film lasts for years, backed by a manufacturer’s warranty. It will not yellow or lift when it is installed properly.

Can I put film on a used car?+

Yes. We will refine the paint first so you are not sealing in existing swirls and scratches. The finish comes back before the film goes on.

Does film really self-heal?+

Yes. Light scratches and swirl marks in the film re-level with heat, whether that is sunlight or warm water.

Should I get full front or full body?+

Full front covers the panels that take the most abuse and is the most popular choice. Full body is for owners who want zero exposure.

Can I add ceramic coating on top?+

Yes, and we recommend it. Film for protection, ceramic for gloss and easy cleaning.

How long will my vehicle be with you?+

It depends on coverage. A full front is quicker than a full body. We will give you a clear timeline with your quote.
